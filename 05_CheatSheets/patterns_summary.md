# 🧠 Patterns Summary

A quick guide to identify **which pattern to use** while solving problems.

---

## 🚀 Core Idea

👉 Most DSA problems are not unique—they follow **repeatable patterns**.

---

## 🔥 Most Important Patterns

### 🟢 Sliding Window

* Subarrays / substrings
* Contiguous elements

👉 Example: longest substring, max sum subarray

---

### 🟢 Two Pointers

* Sorted arrays
* Opposite direction traversal

👉 Example: two sum (sorted), container with water

---

### 🟢 Prefix Sum

* Range queries
* Subarray sums

👉 Example: subarray sum equals k

---

### 🟢 Binary Search

* Sorted data
* “Search space” problems

👉 Example: search in array, binary search on answer

---

### 🟢 Hashing

* Frequency counting
* Fast lookup

👉 Example: two sum, longest sequence

---

## ⚡ Advanced Patterns

### 🔹 Monotonic Stack

* Next greater/smaller
* Range queries

---

### 🔹 Recursion & Backtracking

* Generate all possibilities
* Try all paths

---

### 🔹 Greedy

* Local optimal choice
* Sorting + decision

---

### 🔹 Divide & Conquer

* Break into subproblems
* Merge results

---

### 🔹 Fast & Slow Pointers

* Cycle detection
* Linked list problems

---

### 🔹 Merge Intervals

* Overlapping ranges
* Interval problems

---

## 🎯 How to Identify Pattern?

Ask yourself:

* Is it a subarray? → Sliding Window
* Sorted? → Two Pointers / Binary Search
* Need fast lookup? → Hashing
* All combinations? → Backtracking
* Optimization problem? → Greedy / DP

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

* Trying brute force first
* Not recognizing pattern early
* Overcomplicating solution

---

## 💡 Key Insight

👉 The difference between average and strong problem solvers is:
**pattern recognition speed**

---

## 🧾 Final Tip

Don’t memorize solutions.
Train your brain to recognize patterns.

---

Solve → Observe → Generalize → Repeat 🚀
